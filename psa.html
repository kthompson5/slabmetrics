PSA – Company Profile

Snapshot

Founded: 1991

Headquarters: Santa Ana, California

Website: psacard.com

Submission Portal: Submit a Card

Power Rankings Score: 8.97

Volume
10.0

PSA grades more cards than any other company in the hobby. Their output is unmatched across all sports and TCG categories.

Appearance
8.5

PSA’s slab is iconic—simple, consistent, and instantly recognizable thanks to the bold red label and clear layout.

Turnaround
6.0

Turnaround times vary by service level and demand, ranging from 10 to 65 business days.

Pricing
5.5

PSA's tiered pricing starts around $19 and scales up quickly based on declared card value and speed.

Technology
6.5

Advanced features include Cert Verification, barcodes, and AI-supported card recognition. Blockchain tools are not yet used.

Transparency
7.5

Collectors benefit from population reports and cert lookup tools. PSA is generally transparent but can be slow during backlogs.

Resale Value
9.5

PSA slabs consistently command the highest resale premiums, trusted by collectors and investors alike.

Brand Recognition
10.0

PSA is the most recognized brand in the industry. Their slabs are featured in top auctions and high-profile collections.

Slab Design

PSA slabs are clean, reliable, and familiar. The red header label and standardized format make them instantly recognizable, even from a distance. Simplicity and legacy appeal to many collectors.

Submission & Pricing

PSA offers a robust online submission system with options for bulk, express, and premium levels. Pricing starts at $19/card for low-value items and scales up depending on declared value and service speed.

Transparency & Technology

PSA provides population reports, certification lookups, and uses AI-assisted technology for card recognition. While not the most tech-heavy platform, their systems are reliable and useful.

Resale Performance

PSA slabs dominate resale platforms. Whether vintage or modern, a PSA-graded card is almost always the highest selling version of that card across marketplaces.

Brand Visibility

PSA is a household name in the hobby, featured in major auctions, sponsored events, and high-traffic dealer booths. Their branding is ingrained into card culture.

Pros & Cons

  • Pros: Top resale value, trusted brand, strong infrastructure and population data
  • Cons: Higher pricing tiers, inconsistent turnaround times depending on volume

Final Take

PSA sets the benchmark for grading companies. With a reputation for consistency and value, they are the go-to option for collectors looking to protect, authenticate, and maximize the market potential of their cards.
